Bhajias

A bhajia is any bite-sized vegetable that has been coated in a delicious gram flour batter and then deep-fried to perfection. Also known as pakoras, these bites are very common street food found all over the Indian subcontinent and in plenty of Indian menus around the world.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Bengal gram flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 pinch asafoetida
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • teaspoon thymol seeds, or carom seeds
  • 1 medium red onion

Steps

  1. Mix the gram flour and all the spices with a little water at a time in a bowl, to make a thick batter, slightly thicker than pancake batter. Add salt to taste.
  2. If using cauliflower, separate the florets into bite-sized pieces. The potatoes and onions should be thinly sliced.
  3. Preheat oil and reduce the flame to medium. This will allow the bhajias to cook well both on the outside and inside.
  4. Coat the vegetables well with batter, and then deep-fry till golden.
  5. Drain on paper towels and serve with tamarind chutney or tomato ketchup.
